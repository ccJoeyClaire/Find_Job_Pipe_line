Position: Architectural Intern

Responsibilities

Assist architects and interior designers in design-related work, including early-stage conceptual design development, creation of renderings and design presentation documents, mid-stage technical coordination and drawing production, as well as late-stage material sample selection, on-site inspections, and other related tasks.

Requirements
  • Currently enrolled in a university program, with a major in Architecture or Interior Design (3rd year or above for undergraduate students, or postgraduate students).
  • Possess strong conceptual design and graphic presentation skills; have a basic understanding of the full design process for architecture/interior design and fundamental drawing standards.
  • Have a basic knowledge of architectural/interior materials and technologies, as well as certain communication and coordination abilities.
  • Proficient in essential design software including Rhino, AutoCAD, Photoshop, Revit, and InDesign; have strong English communication skills
  • Passionate about architectural design; possess curiosity, a strong sense of responsibility and teamwork spirit; be proactive, with excellent learning ability and adaptability.
  • Available for a full-time internship of at least 3 months; candidates who can commit to 5 full working days per week are preferred.

环境可持续性

As architects, designers, engineers, and urban planners, we are working together to reduce the carbon impact of the building industry. We are delivering on a twofold pledge to achieve net zero carbon emissions: in our own operations, and in every project we design. SOM is now a net-zero emissions business, and we are targeting net-zero whole life carbon in 100 percent of our active design work by 2040. Our Climate Action Group works across our offices and disciplines to share insight and implement best practices. Building partnerships beyond our firm, the group is defining rigorous standards for low-carbon architecture and construction. We are leading industry-wide initiatives including WorldGBC Advancing Net Zero, RIBA 2030 Climate Challenge, MEP 2040 Committing to Zero, and the UN Environment Programme Global Alliance for Buildings and Construction.
