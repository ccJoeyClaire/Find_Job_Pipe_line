Product Technical Manager

Dyson · 中国 广东省 深圳

职位简介

Overview

Dyson's products operate at the cutting edge of engineering, integrating complex technologies to deliver leading performance and world-class reliability. As we integrate an ever-increasing number of these technologies into our products, the management of the associated complexity becomes key to the success of the project. The Product Technical Manager is a key product leadership position in the Project Leadership Team, partnering the project Design Manager and taking ownership of the overall product technical delivery. They operate as the primary point of contact with the JDM/ODM partners for all technical work-packages.

Detailed Job Scope (Roles & Responsibilities)

Technical Partner to the Design Manager

  • Operating as the 'Technical Product Owner', the Senior Product Technical Manager partners the Design Manager and is accountable for the ultimate delivery of the overall technical maturity of the project.
  • They operate as the primary point of contact between engineering/category teams and external JDM/ODM partners for technical work packages at the product level.


Technical Leadership
  • They assume ownership of product level functional requirements and non-functional attributes and constraints such as safety, regulatory compliance and reliability.
  • They work closely with both the Dyson project leadership (DM/PM) as well as JDM/ODM partner leaders to ensure the technical deliverables at each milestone are appropriately set and the delivery timelines are realistically laid out.
  • With their extensive breadth and depth of technical expertise, they lead complex, product level trade-off discussions to enable the correct engineering solutions for the product across multiple functional teams. These will often require nuanced understanding of complex subjects to enable teams to converge in the presence of conflicting information.
  • Contributes to the larger strategy of technical delivery by architecting and leading initiatives/programs such standardisation of tools, processes and management of stakeholders including approaches to managing risks/issues across the product portfolio.
  • Manage, mentor and coach junior engineers and technical leaders.


Technical Governance
  • They collaborate directly with peers within the project team who would be engineering managers, project managers or subject matter experts at Associate Principal/Principal grades to set out the development strategy for the project.
  • They are accountable for the appropriate and complete ownership of systems, sub-systems and no ambiguities at interfaces amongst engineering teams.
  • They directly report technical readiness of the project to RDD Directors at the Phase Reviews against milestone deliverables prescribed in the Product Creation Framework.
  • They chair and lead project Technical Reviews after each design iteration spanning all engineering functions.


Technical Status/Progress Reporting
  • They periodically review and report technical readiness of the project from the perspective of correctness and completeness of requirements/specifications and closure of technical risk and issues.


Qualifications
  • A first degree in mechanical/mechatronics/electrical/electronics engineering depending on the product/category of products in the portfolio
  • Minimum of 5 years of experience in a product development environment ideally from early-stage development through till the start of mass production. Experience being in a similar role on several projects or an owner of significant product level work packages
  • A combination of deep subject matter expertise within their fundamental engineering disciplines along with a demonstrable, broader appreciation and knowledge of other disciplines allowing them to collaborate and lead cross-functional work-packages essential for the successful delivery of projects. A peer-respected engineer with a reputation for delivering and for resolving complex technical challenges.
  • Experience managing/leading product development through external JDM/ODM partners is highly desirable.
  • A sound knowledge and experience of best practices in technical specifications, risk and issue management.
  • Demonstrable experience of working on complex engineering problems involving cross-functional interactions and deliverables
  • Demonstrable experience of undertaking engineering trade-off analysis and leading related reviews/discussions with the cross-functional stakeholders.
  • An appreciation of systems thinking, systems engineering and project management tools and practices is highly desirable but not essential.
  • Leadership skills - a demonstrable ability to lead cross-functional project teams in the resolution of product level risks and issues.
  • A demonstrable ability to manage stakeholders accountable for product quality, reliability, safety, performance claims and compliance & approvals.
  • An ability to persuade and influence at all levels in pursuit of technical excellence and timely project delivery both internally within Dyson as well as with external partners
  • Experience using Siemens Teamcenter is desirable.
  • Excellent communication and reporting skills, with the ability to report and communicate appropriate information at all levels from engineers to C-level executives.

Dyson offers products across a growing range of areas: floorcare, air purification, robotics, haircare including formulations, lighting, hand drying, and most recently audio. Dyson continues to expand into new areas.

Today, Dyson sells products in more than 80 markets, has 450 Dyson stores worldwide and is available in all major technology and beauty retailers. Dyson has global headquarters in Singapore and major technology campuses in Singapore, the UK, Malaysia, and the Philippines. Its global team of engineers, scientists and software developers are focused on developing technology-enabled products which work better and which people love to use. Key areas of focus have included high-speed electric digital motors, sensing and vision systems, robotics, machine learning and aerodynamics.

Beyond products, to encourage an inventive future, Dyson is also inspiring the next generation of engineers and inventors through the Dyson Institute of Engineering and Technology, the James Dyson Foundation and the James Dyson Award.

The Dyson family applies its problem-solving approach in other fields, and established Dyson Farming in 2012. It is one of the largest farming businesses in the UK, extending to 36,000 acres across Lincolnshire, Oxfordshire, Gloucestershire and Somerset.
