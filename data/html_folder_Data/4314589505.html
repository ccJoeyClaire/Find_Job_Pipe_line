About Club Med

A Global Leading All-Inclusive Vacation Group –

Since Club Med was founded in 1950 and we created the all-inclusive vacation concept, Club Med has been the world leader on its market, and has developed a resolutely upscale, friendly and multicultural spirit. Club Med boasts 68 resorts located in the most beautiful sites in the world, a cruise ship and Luxury Villas & Chalets and, now more than ever, is associated with dreams and happiness.

There are 20,000 Gentle Organizers (G.Os) and Gentle Employees (G.Es) at Club Med, who work in the villages, but also at the Paris, Lyon, Singapore, Shanghai, Rio de Janeiro and Miami headquarters, in the sales offices situated in dozens of countries and in travel agencies.


Why work with us?

Club Med offers the opportunity to broaden our professional scope in amazing destinations all around the world, and to embrace a unique mindset and lifestyle in a company that has always been purposeful and committed to people and environment.

Club Med has always been, and will continue to be, an experience like no other, a life-changing experience.


Title: Business Controller, Greater China

Working Location: Shanghai, China

Your opportunity

As the Business Controller, you will be responsible for
  • To support the financial planning, analysis, and reporting for the Greater China P&L including Mainland China and HK
  • To be responsible the operational follow-up, performance analysis, and financial control of all China resorts.
  • To act as a strategic business partner, providing data-driven insights to support decision-making across commercial and operational functions, ensuring the financial health and growth of the APAC BU.


How you will contribute
  • Commercial Business Control & Analysis
  • Own the budgeting, forecasting, and monthly closing processes for the China Mainland commercial activities, ensuring adherence to Group timelines.
  • Perform in-depth analysis of commercial performance (e.g., channel mix, pricing, marketing ROI) to identify risks, opportunities, and cost optimization levers.
  • Act as a trusted finance business partner to the Commercial team, providing actionable recommendations and supporting business cases for new initiatives, promotions, and strategic projects.
  • Resort Operations Control & Performance Management
  • Lead the monthly closing, forecasting, and budgeting for all China resorts, analyzing and challenging the P&L results to maximize profitability.
  • Work closely with Village Finance Managers to monitor Gross Operating Profit (GOP) and other key operational metrics, driving cost efficiency initiatives.
  • Maintain a comprehensive overview of major resort contracts, alerting stakeholders to financial implications and ensuring compliance.
  • Planning, Budgeting & Group Reporting
  • Drive the implementation and execution of the consolidated budget process for China, aligning commercial ambitions with operational realities.
  • Develop and enhance financial reporting tools (in systems like Hyperion, NAFI, Power BI) to improve efficiency, accuracy, and insight generation for both commercial and operational reporting.
  • Internal Control & Compliance
  • Ensure the thorough application and evolution of Group internal control procedures across all commercial and resort operational activities.
  • Conduct regular control checks and audit missions, detecting anomalies, and submitting actionable recommendations for improvement.
  • Serve as the key contact for internal control topics for China, overseeing the self-assessment process reported to HQ.
  • Projects & Systems
  • Lead or contribute to transversal projects aimed at optimizing the business model, improving daily operations, or launching new ventures.
  • Build financial business cases for new openings and ad-hoc projects, and monitor pre-opening budgets.
  • Follow the evolution of Group IT financial systems and champion their effective implementation within the BU.


Who you are?
  • Essential:
  • Bachelor's degree or higher in Finance, Accounting, or a related field.
  • Minimum of 5 years of experience in finance, business controlling, or internal audit, with a proven track record in financial analysis and strategic planning.
  • Strong technical accounting skills and excellent analytical capabilities.
  • Advanced proficiency in Microsoft Excel; experience with financial planning systems (e.g., Hyperion Planning, Oracle Essbase) is a significant plus.
  • Exceptional communication and stakeholder management skills, with the ability to influence and act as a true business partner to both commercial and operational leaders.
  • Fluent in both English and Mandarin (written and spoken) is mandatory.
  • Preferred:
  • Experience within the hospitality, travel, or retail industries.
  • Proven project management skills and a solution-oriented mindset.
  • Knowledge of French is an advantage but not required.
