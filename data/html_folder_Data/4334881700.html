The British International School Shanghai, Puxi - a Nord Anglia Education school and the world’s leading private school’s organisation is recruiting a Head of Business and Economics.

At Nord Anglia Education, our teachers inspire students to achieve more than they ever imagined possible. As part of our global family, you’ll deliver transformational learning focused on academic excellence, creativity, wellbeing, and international connectedness. We empower our educators to innovate, embrace change, and shape their roles in a fast-growing, dynamic environment.

The teacher is expected to support curriculum implementation, student engagement, and innovation within a dynamic, inclusive international environment.

If you have a global mindset, enjoy thinking outside the box and are prepared to go above and beyond to find solutions, then a career at Nord Anglia is for you.

The Role

As Head of Business and Economics, your focus will be to:

  • Lead initiatives to boost student participation and school spirit.
  • Support enrichment activities and school events.
  • Strengthen parental engagement through clear communication and progress updates.
  • Deliver dynamic, inclusive, and effective lessons.
  • Foster creativity, problem-solving, and design thinking.
  • Use data-driven strategies to support diverse learning needs.
  • Integrate digital tools to enhance learning experiences.
  • Serve as a Form Tutor and contribute to the IB curriculum.
  • Develop and implement curriculum-aligned lesson plans.
  • Ensure rigorous assessment and timely feedback.
  • Contribute to curriculum innovation and quality assurance.
  • Engage in continuous professional learning.
  • Reflect on practice to improve teaching effectiveness.
  • Uphold school values and contribute to a positive school culture.

The Successful Candidate Will Possess
  • Recognised teaching qualification (e.g. PGCE, QTS, or equivalent).
  • Experience of delivering a UK National Curriculum in the UK or overseas.
  • Hold a current Enhanced Criminal Records Bureau Disclosure for all countries lived in.
  • Compliance with visa requirements for working, including a minimum bachelor’s degree, at least 2 years of working experience as a teacher, as well as non-criminal records valid for the last 10 years.
  • A commitment to safeguarding and promoting the welfare of all pupils, and a willingness to undertake appropriate child protection training when required.
