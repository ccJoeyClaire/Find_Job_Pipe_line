职位简介

About CRU:

CRU International is a leading provider of business intelligence and consulting services in the metals, mining, and fertilizer industries. With over 50 years of experience, we offer valuable insights and analysis that help our clients make informed decisions in an ever-changing global market.


About the role:

CRU seeks an Aluminium Assets Analyst to deliver asset-focused subscription services and convert complex datasets into clear, client-ready insight. The role will maintain aluminium asset models and datasets, conduct primary and secondary research with industry contacts, and translate findings into market-shaping analysis and timely service deliverables. The analyst will support Service Leads, collaborate across the Assets and broader analysis teams to ensure consistent, high-quality delivery, and help identify commercial opportunities to grow the service.


Key Responsibilities:
  • Build and maintain a strong understanding of aluminium commodity markets and the assets within remit
  • Contribute to asset-level analysis and modelling for the Service(s) under remit
  • Maintain, organise and update the data series and aluminium asset models that form part of customer deliverables
  • Conduct primary and secondary research, including developing and managing relationships with market, industry and institutional contacts
  • Translate research and datasets into clear, market-shaping analysis and timely client deliverables
  • Attend industry conferences and events to build networks and represent CRU
  • Support client-facing activities including meetings, demos and webinars
  • Respond to internal requests for data and asset-related information and help identify commercial opportunities to grow the service


Qualifications:
  • Essential: Bachelor’s degree from a top-tier institution
  • Preferred: Further education or certification in a subject relevant to commodity research (e.g., geology, economics, engineering)


Skills & Experience:
  • Strong ability to gather and critically analyse both “soft” (qualitative) and “hard” (quantitative) data to identify trends and drivers and generate actionable insights
  • Highly numerate with experience interpreting, manipulating and managing large datasets and understanding relevant mathematical concepts and processes
  • Educational background in a quantitative subject
  • Experience conducting primary and secondary research and engaging with industry contacts
  • Able to collaborate across global analysis teams, share best practice, and balance personal initiative with team-based working
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills for client deliverables and presentations


What We Offer:
  • Competitive salary and flexible benefits package.
  • Opportunities for professional growth and development as part of a global company.
  • A collaborative and supportive work environment.
  • The chance to work with industry-leading experts and over a diverse range of topics and projects.
